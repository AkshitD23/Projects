"Creative Schools: The Grassroots Revolution That's Transforming Education" by Ken Robinson provides a timely and important analysis of education in the world today. The book follows a widely viewed TED Talk from 2006 in which Robinson poses an important question to the audience, "Do Schools Kill Creativity"? Robinson addresses this question in the book by pointing out that in many ways, education is done in largely the same manner around the world. Although his primary concern is that of American education, he points to global examples of how education is changing. The way things have always been done since the Industrial Revolution and England to NCLB under President Bush have offered little true reform.
